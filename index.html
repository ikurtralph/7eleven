Overview of Payment Options for NBI Clearance

There are two primary categories to pay for your NBI Clearance fees:

  1. Retail Payment - through 7-Eleven CLIQQ

  2. Digital Payment - via Mobile Wallets like GCash, Maya, and others

Both options start with a transaction reference number, which is generated after you book your NBI online appointment.

Option 1: 7-Eleven CLIQQ

7-Eleven offers a convenient and accessible method for NBI payment using its CLIQQ self-service kiosks found in most branches across the country.

How It Works:

  • Visit any 7-Eleven branch with a CLIQQ kiosk

  • Tap "Bills Payment" → Choose "NBI" or scan the QR code if supported

  • Enter your reference number

  • Proceed to the cashier and pay in cash

  • Get a receipt and wait for payment confirmation via SMS or email

Pros:

  • Widely available across the Philippines

  • No need for a bank account or app

  • Accepts cash - great for the unbanked

  • Receipt issued instantly

Cons:

  • Must visit a physical store

  • Possible long queues

  • Cash-only, no card payments

  • Payment cut-off times apply for same-day posting

Option 2: Mobile Wallets (GCash, Maya, ShopeePay, etc.)

Mobile wallets offer a fully digital way to pay for your NBI fees. They're linked to your phone and allow instant cashless payments 24/7.

How It Works:

  • Open your mobile wallet (GCash, Maya, ShopeePay, etc.)

  • Tap "Pay Bills" or "Government" → Choose NBI

  • Input your reference number and amount due

  • Confirm and authorize the payment

  • Receive confirmation instantly via app and SMS

Pros:

  • Completely contactless

  • Instant posting (in most cases)

  • Pay anytime, anywhere - no need to travel

  • Track your transactions in-app

  • Often includes promos or rebates

Cons:

  • Requires internet and mobile data

  • Wallet must be funded in advance

  • Transaction limits may apply for basic users

  • Possible downtimes during peak hours or system maintenance

Key Takeaways

  • Use 7-Eleven if you prefer cash payments and don't mind walking to a nearby store

  • Choose mobile wallets for faster, digital, and contactless payments from home

  • Both options require a valid reference number from your NBI online booking

  • Always keep proof of payment for tracking and troubleshooting

  • Payment confirmation may take up to 24 hours (7-Eleven), while digital wallets are typically faster

Things to Remember Before You Pay

  • Make sure your reference number is correct before entering it

  • Pay only the exact amount due as stated on your NBI portal

  • Don't delete payment confirmations or app receipts

  • Always double-check your internet connection and mobile wallet balance if paying digitally

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can I use a credit or debit card at 7-Eleven for NBI payments?

No. 7-Eleven only accepts cash payments for NBI Clearance through CLIQQ.

Which mobile wallets are accepted for NBI payments?

GCash, Maya, and ShopeePay are among the supported wallets. Always check your NBI portal for the latest accepted channels.

How do I know if my NBI payment was successful?

You'll receive a confirmation SMS or email from NBI. You can also check the payment status by logging into your NBI portal account.

What if I paid but didn't receive confirmation?

Wait up to 24 hours. If there's still no update, contact NBI customer service with your payment receipt.

Can I pay someone else's NBI clearance fee through my mobile wallet?

Yes. As long as you input the correct reference number, you can pay on behalf of another person.

Conclusion

But if you value speed, convenience, and cashless payments, mobile wallets clearly take the lead.

Both options are secure, reliable, and fully integrated into the NBI Clearance Renewal Online system. Just remember: your payment method should work for you - not the other way around.
